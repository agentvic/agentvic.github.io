## Conflict resolution for individuals. 

Sometimes we have internal conflicts that need to be resolved in order to move on to a new challenge in our lives. An internal conflict can manifest in many ways, and can limit growth if ignored or avoided.

## Interpersonal conflict resolution.

Interpersonal relationships can be tricky and begin to dissolve when underlying conflict goes unresolved. This conflict can show up in marriage, friendship, sibling relationships and the relationship between parent and child.

## Corporate facilitation.

When working in a team setting there are many types of conflict that can arise and avoiding them can in the long run be detrimental to the culture of the company. Working through the conflict as a team with a neutral party guiding the process, is a great way to get your team members on the same page. This will open the communication channels, and increase your team’s productivity levels as they work together to be more effective, than before. A whole is greater than the sum of its parts, and what better way to become a whole than to resolve conflicts keeping you apart.
